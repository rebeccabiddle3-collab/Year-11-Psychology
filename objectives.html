What you'll develop

Unit Objectives

By the end of Units 1 & 2, you will be able to use the following cognitive verbs across a range of psychology topics.

1
Describe — provide characteristics and features of a concept, idea, process or phenomenon.
2
Discuss — provide points for and/or against; consider multiple perspectives with supporting evidence.
3
Explain — make a concept clear; show cause-and-effect relationships and give reasons for processes or outcomes.
4
Apply — use knowledge in a specific context; calculate unknown quantities and identify features from data.
5
Analyse — examine in detail; identify trends, patterns, relationships, limitations and uncertainty in data.
6
Interpret — draw meaning from data; deduce, infer, extrapolate and justify using scientific reasoning.
7
Evaluate — make a judgement based on criteria; weigh evidence for and against; assess the value of claims.
8
Investigate — plan, conduct and communicate a psychological inquiry using appropriate research methodology.

Science as a Human Endeavour (SHE)

SHE dot points appear throughout the syllabus and are marked SHE on the Tracking page. They ask you to consider the broader context of psychological knowledge.

Development of psychology — how techniques and understanding have evolved over time (e.g. phrenology → neuroimaging).
Application & implications — how psychological knowledge informs policy, law, education and clinical practice.
Nature & practice — ethical principles in research, limitations of evidence, cultural contexts of knowledge.
Sociocultural influences — how social, cultural and historical factors shape psychological theories and diagnoses.

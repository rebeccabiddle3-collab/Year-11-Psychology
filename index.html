Unit Summaries

An overview of what you'll study in Units 1 and 2 this year.

Unit 1

Individual Development

Brain · Cognition · Consciousness & Sleep

Explore how psychology describes and explains the developing brain, cognitive processes across the lifespan, and the nature of human consciousness, attention and sleep.

  • T1The Role of the Brain — mind-body problem, neuroimaging, nervous system, neuron structure, language areas15 hrs
  • T2Cognitive Development — plasticity, myelination, synaptic pruning, attachment, Piaget, Vygotsky, information processing15 hrs
  • T3Human Consciousness & Sleep — arousal, attention, EEG/EMG/EOG, sleep stages, theories, disorders & treatments15 hrs

Unit 2

Individual Behaviour

Intelligence · Diagnosis · Disorders · Emotion & Motivation

Investigate how intelligence is conceptualised and measured, how psychological disorders are diagnosed and treated, and how emotion and motivation shape individual behaviour.

  • T1Intelligence — psychometric approach, IQ tests, Sternberg, Gardner, Goleman, twin studies10 hrs
  • T2Diagnosis — adaptive vs maladaptive, concepts of normality, DSM-5-TR, ICD-11, disorder categories10 hrs
  • T3Psychological Disorders & Treatment — biopsychosocial model, GAD, phobias, psychotherapy, pharmacotherapy, placebo15 hrs
  • T4Emotion & Motivation — two-factor theory, appraisal, limbic system, wellbeing, mindfulness, flow, goal-setting10 hrs
